## The basics (every squad)

- **Training suit.** Snug, chlorine-resistant polyester lasts months; fashion swimwear lasts weeks. Girls: one-piece training suit. Boys: jammers or training trunks, not baggy shorts.
- **Goggles that fit.** One pair is fine for training. Get the fit right: gentle suction on the eye socket without the strap doing the work.
- **Club hat.** Keeps hair out of eyes and officials happy. Available through the [club kit page](/club/kit).
- **Drinks bottle.** On poolside, every session, full. See [Nutrition](/athlete-hub/nutrition).
- **Towel and a warm layer** for after training. A hoodie or dryrobe-style coat in winter.

## Squad-by-squad

**Dolphins & Sharks:** that's it. One of each. You don't need a mesh kit bag full of gear at this stage.

**Development & Improvement:** add **fins** (short training fins, not snorkelling flippers) and a **kickboard and pull buoy** if your pool doesn't provide them. A spare pair of goggles in the bag is worth having once galas start, but one good pair for sessions is enough day to day.

**Challenge and above:** the full training bag.

- **Fins** for kick power and speed-assisted technique work
- **Pull buoy** to isolate the arms and teach body position
- **Paddles** for feel and strength in the catch; only the size your coach approves (oversized paddles load the shoulders; see [Injury prevention](/athlete-hub/injury-prevention))
- **Centre snorkel** for technique work without stopping to breathe
- **Mesh bag** to carry it all and let it drain
- **Spare goggles** in the bag once you're racing regularly

**Land training (Improvement and above):** supportive trainers, shorts/leggings and a t-shirt, plus a resistance band if issued. See [Land training](/athlete-hub/land-training).

## Racing kit

What you pack depends on how often you're racing.

**First few galas (Dolphins, Sharks, early Development):** your normal training kit is fine. Club hat, goggles, towel, bottle, warm layer. A clean suit if you want one, but don't go buying extras for the sake of it.

**Regular competitors (Challenge and above):** a **spare pair of goggles** in the gala bag is sensible; straps do snap at the worst moment. A **racing suit** separate from your training suit helps if you're in the pool a lot, but under-12s don't need a tech suit. A fresh ordinary suit is fine. From county level, talk to your coach before spending serious money on a tech suit.

**At any gala:** **T-shirt, shorts and poolside shoes** for between races. Staying warm between swims helps. See the full packing list in our [Competing guide](/compete/guide).

## What not to buy

- **Oversized paddles.** Shoulder trouble waiting to happen.
- **Tech suits for young swimmers.** Expensive and unnecessary.
- **Doubles of everything before you need them.** One good set of kit beats a bag full of spares you never use.
- **Gadgets** (smart goggles, lap counters, tempo trainers). Fun, but nothing a coach asks for at this level.
- **Anything you can't name a purpose for.** Every item in the bag should have a job.
